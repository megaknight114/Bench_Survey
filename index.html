Perception of Online Texts: Credibility, Shareability, and Intent

Institution: Fudan University
Principal Investigators: Zonghuan Xu, Xingjun Ma

A. Informed Consent

Quick Overview

  • You will answer a short background questionnaire and read one short text.
  • You will then answer a few questions based on your impression as a reader.
  • There are no right or wrong answers.
  • Estimated time: 3–5 minutes.

Invitation & Purpose

You are invited to take part in a research study on how people perceive the credibility, shareability, and perceived intent of short texts. Some texts may be true, misleading, or fabricated. The goal is to better understand how misinformation may be perceived and shared.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation is completely voluntary. You may refuse to participate or stop at any time without penalty or negative consequences.

Benefits

There may be no direct personal benefit. Results may help inform research and governance related to misinformation risks.

Privacy & Data Use

  • We do not collect direct identifiers such as your name, student ID, or national ID.
  • We collect your responses and basic background information for research analysis.
  • Data will be stored securely with access limited to authorized researchers.
  • Results will be reported in aggregated form without identifying individuals.
  • Your data may be used for this study and related academic research under ethical oversight.
