Text Perception Study: Credibility, Sharing, and Intent

Institution: Fudan University
Principal Investigators: Zonghuan Xu, Xingjun Ma

A. Informed Consent

Quick Overview

  • You will answer a short background questionnaire and read one short text about 500 words.
  • You will then answer a few questions based on your first impression.
  • No right or wrong answers. Please respond naturally.
  • Estimated time: 3–5 minutes.

Invitation & Purpose

You are invited to take part in a research study on how people perceive the credibility, shareability, and perceived intent of short texts encountered online. Some texts may be true, misleading, or fabricated. We study everyday reading reactions and how certain texts may be perceived and shared.

How to Answer (Important)

Please answer as you would in daily life. Do not overthink and do not treat this as a test. We are interested in your natural judgment. Also, please do not try to guess what the researchers want or who wrote the text. Just respond to what you read.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation is completely voluntary. You may refuse to participate or stop at any time without penalty or negative consequences.

Risks & Discomfort

Some texts may contain sensitive or controversial claims. You may stop at any time if you feel uncomfortable.

Benefits

There may be no direct personal benefit. Results may help inform research and governance related to misinformation risks.

Privacy & Data Use

  • We do not collect direct identifiers such as your name, student ID, or national ID.
  • We collect your responses and basic background information for research analysis.
  • Data will be stored securely with access limited to authorized researchers.
  • Results will be reported in aggregated form without identifying individuals.
  • Your data may be used for this study and related academic research under ethical oversight.
