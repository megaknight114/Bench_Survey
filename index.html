Online Reading Survey

Institution: Fudan University
Principal Investigators: Zonghuan Xu, Xingjun Ma

A. Informed Consent

Quick Overview

  • You will answer a short background questionnaire.
  • You will read five short texts (about 500 words each), presented one at a time.
  • After each text, you will answer a few brief questions based on your first impression.
  • There are no right or wrong answers. Please respond naturally.
  • Estimated time: 15–20 minutes.

Invitation & Purpose

You will complete a short background questionnaire, read one short text (similar to something you might see online), and then answer a few questions about your first impression—for example, how believable it feels, whether you would share it, and whether it seems to have a strong intent to influence readers. Some texts may contain inaccurate information; others may be accurate.

How to Answer (Important)

Please answer as you would in daily life. Do not overthink and do not treat this as a test. We are interested in your natural judgment. Also, please do not try to guess what the researchers want or who wrote the text. Just respond to what you read.

Participation, Risks & Benefits

  • Your participation is completely voluntary.
  • Some texts may contain sensitive or controversial claims. You may stop at any time if you feel uncomfortable.
  • There may be no direct personal benefit. Results may help inform research related to misinformation risks.

Privacy & Data Use

  • We do not collect direct identifiers such as your name, student ID, or national ID.
  • We collect your responses and basic background information for research analysis.
  • Data will be stored securely with access limited to authorized researchers.
  • Results will be reported in aggregated form without identifying individuals.
  • Your data may be used for this study and related academic research under ethical oversight.

Confirmations

This code is self-chosen and does not need to contain personal identifiers. If you participate more than once, please keep the same code. It will only be used to link repeated submissions for statistical analysis.
